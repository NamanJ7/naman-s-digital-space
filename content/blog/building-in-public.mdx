For a long time I kept my projects to myself until they were "ready." They
rarely got ready. Here's what changed when I started sharing earlier.

## Feedback compounds

The sooner an idea meets reality, the cheaper it is to fix. A rough draft
shown to one thoughtful person beats a perfect draft shown to no one.

## Shipping is a habit, not an event

Small, frequent releases keep momentum. The goal isn't a big launch — it's a
steady rhythm:

1. Build the smallest useful version.
2. Put it in front of someone.
3. Learn, adjust, repeat.

## It's mostly about showing up

> You don't have to be great to start, but you have to start to be great.

Most of the value comes from simply continuing. That's the whole secret, and
it's also the hard part.
